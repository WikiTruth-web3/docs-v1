### Society and Human Nature: Why is Justice Silent?

![User Groups](/docs/user-group.svg)

Human society's desire for justice is eternal, yet when individuals face powerful injustice or criminal forces, they often fall into collective silence. Evidence Market deeply understands the complex human nature behind this.

**1. The Tear Between Moral Conscience and Fear**: Insiders desire justice but are bound by fear—fear of retaliation, fear of loss, fear of the unknown. The conflict between morality and realistic interests is the first high wall preventing the truth from surfacing.

**2. The Lack of Anonymity and Security Guarantees**: Under a centralized power system, whistleblower identity information is extremely easily leaked, leading to retaliation. A truly secure, anonymous, and technically untraceable channel is a necessary prerequisite for releasing conscience.

**3. Incentives for Returns and Actions**: Actions of justice driven purely by morality are difficult to sustain. When providing truth can yield reasonable returns, the act of justice transforms from an individual sacrifice into a sustainable social driving force.

**4. The Irreversible Energy of Truth**: The truth is the terminator of all lies. Once key criminal facts are published, no matter how overwhelming the power, its foundation will be shaken.

**5. Bridging the Gap Between Justice and Reality**: Most people yearn for justice but are often suppressed by reality. Evidence Market provides a safe channel and economic support to unleash the courage of those "willing but lacking the power" to uphold justice.

### The Criminal's Mindset: The Spiral of Fear and Cost

Evidence Market deeply perceives the psychological weaknesses of criminals, turning their fear into leverage to uphold justice.

**1. The Absolute Fear of Exposure**: What criminals fear most is not the law itself, but the complete exposure of the truth—which means the collapse of social status, wealth, and reputation, followed by legal sanctions and public trial.

**2. The Cost of Delayed Disclosure**: To maintain the "illusion", criminals might be willing to pay a high price to delay the publication of the truth. Evidence Market's "increasing time fee" mechanism precisely captures this psychology, making covering up the truth an unsustainable economic black hole.

**3. The Shattering of Fluke Mentality**: Criminals always think the truth can be buried forever. But the long-term publication expectation set in Evidence Market's mechanism will shatter this fluke.

**4. The Disintegration of Internal Trust**: Criminal syndicates are filled with mistrust internally. Evidence Market becomes a sword hanging over all accomplices—you never know who will turn you into a Evidence Blind Box for a huge reward, thus detonating internal game theory.

**5. The Deterrence of Unknown Authenticity**: Even if unsure of the authenticity of the evidence in the Evidence Blind Box, the uncertainty of "what if it's real?" is enough to make criminals restless, prompting them to pay a price to verify its authenticity.

Evidence Market leverages the scales of justice through the power of code precisely by anchoring and amplifying these complex human and social games.
